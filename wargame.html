Project Overview

The Medical Evacuation Wargame represents a groundbreaking collaboration between UC Berkeley, Stanford University, and the US Army to revolutionize military medical training. This sophisticated Unity 3D simulation creates realistic medical evacuation scenarios that challenge trainees with dynamic, unpredictable situations they may encounter in the field.

Currently deployed and actively used by the Medvac course, this simulation has proven its value in preparing military personnel for high-stakes medical evacuation operations. The system combines cutting-edge artificial intelligence with immersive 3D environments to create training scenarios that adapt to user decisions and provide realistic challenges.

Key Objectives

  • Develop realistic medical evacuation training scenarios for military personnel
  • Implement adversarial AI using reinforcement learning to create dynamic challenges
  • Provide a safe, controlled environment for practicing high-risk medical procedures
  • Enable repeatable training scenarios with varying difficulty levels
  • Support multi-user collaboration and team-based training exercises

Technologies Used

Unity 3D C# Python Reinforcement Learning Neural Networks PyTorch Machine Learning

Active Deployment

This simulation is currently being used by the Medvac course to train military personnel in medical evacuation procedures. The real-world application validates the effectiveness of our approach and demonstrates the practical value of advanced simulation technology in military training.

Technical Implementation

Adversarial AI System

The core innovation of this project lies in its sophisticated AI system that creates realistic opposition and dynamic challenges. Using reinforcement learning algorithms, the AI adapts to user behavior and creates increasingly complex scenarios that test decision-making under pressure.

Unity 3D Environment

The simulation leverages Unity's powerful 3D engine to create immersive environments that accurately represent battlefield conditions. High-fidelity graphics and realistic physics ensure that trainees experience scenarios that closely mirror real-world conditions.

Multi-Agent Collaboration

The system supports multiple users working together, simulating the team-based nature of actual medical evacuation operations. This collaborative aspect is crucial for training effective communication and coordination skills.
